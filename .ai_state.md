## 🧠 Dev Notes & Hypotheses

- **2026-05-27**: The 2026-05-18 Stage 2 output was degenerate. The old `s2_fingerprint.py` wrote a constant (`DIMS[i].options[0]`) down every column for all 35 dicts to game the "40% filled" acceptance bar — every dictionary identical, so any tree would be a meaningless star. Rebuilt it as a real streaming extractor over `../csl-orig/v02/<code>/<code>.txt` (32 of 35 dicts cloned locally).
- Extractor design: samples ≤4000 entries/dict (conventions are global, so first-N is fine); 21 auto dims; 9 left for annotation (Patel 1-7, sandhi 8, MBh-edition 16). Margin-based confidence capped at 0.9. A **variance check flags constant columns** instead of hiding them.
- Result: 589/1050 cells (56%), **19 informative dims**, **2 flagged constants (15, 18)**. Validated against known facts for WIL / MW / PWG / VCP / SKD — all strong signals correct (see EXECUTION_LOG 2026-05-27 block).
- External inputs **unchanged as of 2026-05-27** (confirmed with M.G.): no Patel 2016 PDF, no classical manuals, no Matomo access. So Patel dims stay unauto-fillable.
- Note: KCH (Kochergina) is NOT in the 35-dict L0 subset.
- **2026-05-27 preview validation**: built the first REAL convention cladogram (`scripts/L0/preview_tree.py` → `data/L0/preview/`) on the 19 informative dims × 32 dicts, missing-aware Gower + Jaccard, UPGMA + NJ. **All 6 known lineages came out tighter than the global mean (0.433)**: VCP/SKD=0.000, AP90/AP=0.210, CAE/CCS=0.222, Petersburg-German=0.353, Wilson=0.370, MW72/MW=0.389. Nearest-neighbour pairs recover AP90↔AP, VCP↔SKD, PW↔SCH, CAE↔SHS. So the 19 auto dims already carry real phylogenetic signal even before the Patel dims are added. Surprises to revisit after annotation: YAT↔STC (d=0.059), MW grouping with GRA, BHS near AP90. NOT the gated Stage 3/4 (preflight needs cells_unknown=0).

- **2026-05-27 YAT↔STC chase**: investigated why YAT (Yates 1846 Skt-Eng, a Wilson digest) and STC (Stchoupak 1932 Skt-Fra, a Petersburg digest) were nearest convention-neighbours (d=0.059). Verdict: **genuine, not artifact** — both are minimalist bilingual glossaries (0% `<ls>` citations, 0% etymology, no cognate tags, abbreviated grammar, segmented headwords). **Methodological lesson: the convention fingerprint measures editorial REGISTER, which converges across unrelated lineages (homoplasy) — convention-distance ≠ descent.** This argues for combining it with the lemma-overlap Jaccard (which *does* capture descent: WIL→YAT was 91% containment in sanhw1). Needs a stated caveat in the paper. The chase also found + fixed a real bug: `{%...%}` italic-gloss markup was miscounted as a foreign-language tag, inflating dims 21 & 27 for every bilingual dict. Fixed; re-ran; 6/6 lineages still cohere; Wilson group tightened 0.37→0.30, Cappeller 0.22→0.11. Diagnostic kept at `scripts/L0/diag_compare.py`.
- Known under-detection (not yet fixed): YAT lightly numbers senses with mid-line `1.` (e.g. `{#(aH)#} 1. {%m.%}`) which the arabic-numbering regex (line-start only) misses → YAT dim11=unnumbered is slightly off. Low impact.
