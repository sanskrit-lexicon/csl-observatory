H1 — does sense granularity inflate over time?

Each point is one dictionary: x = publication year, y = mean sense-units per entry over its whole corpus; colour = lexicographic family. Generated by scripts/lexico/h1_analysis.py from csl-orig.

Finding. Granularity clusters by FAMILY, not year: enumerating traditions (Apte, Benfey) pack many sense-units/entry; lumping traditions (Monier-Williams, Petersburg, indigenous) ~1. The year-trend is essentially flat (Pearson r=0.06) -> H1 (pure temporal inflation) is NOT supported; sense granularity is a tradition / marking-style trait, which Paper L must therefore CONTROL FOR. Overall year-trend is weak (Pearson r = 0.06); the variance is captured by family (means: Benfey 2.53, Apte 2.35, Monier-Williams 2.06, Wilson 1.8, Cappeller 1.35, Petersburg 1.15, indigenous 1.09).
