H1 (de-confounded) — sense granularity on a fixed 30-noun panel

Same 30 common nouns measured in every dictionary (homonym blocks aggregated), removing the headword-splitting confound. x = year, y = mean sense-units per panel word, colour = family. scripts/lexico/h1_panel.py.

Finding. De-confounded (same words, every dict): the year-trend is flat over the whole set (Pearson r = 0.01). A weak positive correlation appears among the 5 comparable enumerating dicts (r = 0.56), but at n=5 it is not significant and is convention-confounded — the earliest dictionary, Wilson 1832, is already among the most finely enumerated (11 senses/word), and mid-century PWG looks low only because its coarse <div> marking undercounts. No support for temporal sense-inflation; granularity is governed by lexicographic tradition and marking convention, not date. Family means: Monier-Williams 56.17, Apte 14.04, Wilson 11.11, Benfey 8, Cappeller 4.37, indigenous 3.54, Petersburg 3.26.
