# Silver validation of the inferred labels (OBS-T)

_Generated by `scripts/obs_t_silver.py`. A human-free reliability bound for the empirical-cluster fallback labels: on form events where the csl-orig headword join succeeded (the more reliable signal), how often would the fallback heuristic have given the same component? This is a silver standard pending the human gold sample, not a replacement for it._

|---|---:|
| testable overlap (join-derived form events, heuristic non-abstaining) | 6,969 |

Where the two signals overlap they agree only **0.1%** of the time â€” and that near-zero number is a structural finding, not noise. The join assigns the component by **location** (a headword typo â†’ `headword`), while the empirical heuristic assigns it by **edit type** (`typo` â†’ `orthography`). They disagree because they measure **different axes**. The consequence: the inferred fallback was filing *type*-axis values (`orthography`, `encoding`) into the *location*-axis component column. On the trustworthy derived (location) labels the typology is led by **sense, headword, markup, citation** â€” not orthography, which is almost entirely a fallback artifact. **Recommendation:** derive the microstructure (location) typology from derived labels only; keep edit-type (orthography/encoding/diacritic) in the ERRANT/OCR crosswalk where it belongs; label join-failures `unattributed` rather than guessing a component.
