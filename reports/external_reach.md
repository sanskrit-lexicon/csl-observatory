**The stars-vs-clones gap is the finding.** The org collects ~103 stars in total, yet its core repositories are cloned **6,923 times in a single 14-day window**. CDSL is consumed as *infrastructure* — cloned, mirrored, and served through the Cologne website — not favourited on GitHub. Star count badly understates reach.

## Reading

- **Reach is real but invisible on GitHub's vanity surface.** ~103 stars, yet 6,923 clones in two weeks and 10 named downstream projects (PyCDSL, Ambuda, the Digital Pali Dictionary, StarDict-Sanskrit, Ashtadhyayi.com, ...). The audience is builders and scholars pulling data, not stargazers.
- **The dependents are the strongest scholar-facing evidence** — each is a URL-checkable project that chose CDSL as its lexical backbone.
- **Citations are under-counted here by design**; a systematic Scholar / OpenAlex sweep (API-gated) is the natural G6 extension.
- **Fix the OBS-T DOI before the paper ships** — the current DOI points at someone else's work.
