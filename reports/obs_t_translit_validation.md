**Finding — the cfr romanization mixes HK and SLP1 conventions.** The HK disagreements are not random: they concentrate on tokens that follow the **SLP1** convention `ñ = Y` (e.g. `paYcatriMSat` = pañcatriṃśat) rather than HK `ñ = J`. Our converter now maps `Y → ñ` to match the corpus; the pure-HK reference renders it `ẏ`, which is why these remain "disagreements" against a naive-HK baseline. A residual ambiguity is the **sibilant swap** (HK `z=ś, S=ṣ` vs SLP1 `S=ś, z=ṣ`): convention-mixed tokens can transpose ś/ṣ invisibly. This affects the *encoding-level* confusion signal for a small minority of romanized form tokens, not the microstructure component typology (the main claim) — documented as a limitation. SLP1 and Devanagari agree with the reference at ~100%.

*Validation artifact; the pipeline itself remains stdlib-only (`indic_transliteration` is used only here, to validate).*
