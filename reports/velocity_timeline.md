## Reading

- **Throughput is real but narrow-shouldered.** 12,313 commits and 5,460 issues over 13 years, yet the busiest year drew only **11 distinct authors** (2021). The activity curve is driven by volume per person, not by a growing contributor base — the same concentration the bus-factor finding quantifies (`reports/bus_factor.md`).
- **Pull requests barely register.** Only 713 PRs in 13 years. They first appear in **2019** and stay in single digits annually until **2026**, when 677 of the 713 lifetime PRs (94%) landed. The org runs on direct commits and issues; review-based contribution is only now emerging.
- **A correction-campaign signature, not steady-state maintenance.** The open-issue backlog climbed to a peak of 1,743 in 2025 — after a record 1,178 issues were opened org-wide that year, driven by `csl-orig`'s git-based correction workflow — then fell sharply to 806 in 2026 as 1,447 issues were closed. Activity comes in correction waves, not a flat hum.
