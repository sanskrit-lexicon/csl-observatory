# Issue-taxonomy adoption & conformance

_Generated by `scripts/taxonomy_adoption.py` from `observatory/site/src/data/issues.csv` (offline, reproducible). 5,324 issues across 13 years (2014–2026); 89 pull requests excluded — the taxonomy applies to issues._

Conformance = exactly one **type** label **and** exactly one **severity** label **and** a **milestone**, with no stray labels — the rule stated in the org `CLAUDE.md`. Labels were applied retroactively by the runbook, so these are coverage figures across the historical issue base, bucketed by the year each issue was opened.

## Reading

- Of 5,324 issues, **89% carry a type label** and **63% are fully conformant** (one type + one severity + a milestone). The taxonomy is broadly but not completely applied across the 13-year corpus.
- **324 issues carry more than one type label** (6%) — a conformance violation under the one-type rule, worth a cleanup pass.
- **999 issues still carry a stray label** (19%); see the inventory above for delete/fold candidates.

See [`reports/issue_typology_annual`](../observatory/site/src/data/issue_typology_annual.csv) for the type-mix composition by year (e.g. the steep rise of `text-correction` and the 2025 surge in `link-target`).

*Object of analysis: GitHub issues and their labels — in scope per `docs/BOUNDARY_RULES.md`.*
