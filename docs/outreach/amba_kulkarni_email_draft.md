Dear Prof. Kulkarni,

I hope this finds you well. I am Mārcis Gasūns, Kandidat Nauk in Indology, working on a long-term quantitative study of the Cologne Digital Sanskrit Lexicon (CDSL) project. I write with two related requests.

**Background.** Over the past months, my colleagues and I have been building an open observatory for CDSL at <https://sanskrit-lexicon.github.io/csl-observatory/>, which produces empirical measurements of all 41+ Cologne dictionaries — lemma overlap matrices, inheritance cladograms, contributor specialisation, microstructure typology, etc. The work is fully open: code at <https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/csl-observatory>, all data downloadable under CC-BY-SA, no institutional funding. Our planned outputs include a series of articles at WSC 2028 and onward, a scientific monograph, a practical Sanskrit-lexicography manual, and ultimately a new corpus-based Sanskrit-Russian dictionary.

**Request 1 — Apte's *Practical Sanskrit-Hindi* dictionary data.** In our analysis of derivational and frequency markers in Apte's lexicographic tradition (AP90 1890, AP 1957), we have observed that the published English versions encode subentries (Caus./Pass./Desid./Inten.) but very little explicit derivation or frequency annotation. We understand from colleagues that V. S. Apte's *Practical Sanskrit-Hindi Dictionary* contains substantially richer derivation data, and that this dictionary is **not** part of the CDSL set. If you have a digital version of this dictionary (or know who does), would you be willing to share it for inclusion in our analysis? Any format is workable — XML, plain text, scanned OCR output. We would credit you appropriately as the source-of-data contributor.

**Request 2 — Co-authorship on the subentry-analysis paper.** Our Article 9 in the planned series is titled *Subentry microstructure in Sanskrit dictionaries: a verb-derivation matrix across 35 CDSL dicts*, targeted at the *International Journal of Lexicography* in 2030. It analyses verbal derivational coverage (Caus., Pass., Desid., Inten., Den., Periphr., preverb-prefixed forms, participle compounds) across the corpus. Your work on Sanskrit computational morphology and the Hyderabad Sanskrit Computational Linguistics tradition would substantially strengthen the paper. Would you consider joining as co-author? The work involves:

- Reviewing our extraction methodology for verbal subentries
- Contributing domain expertise on Pāṇinian categorisation of derivational types (kṛt vs taddhita vs uṇādi)
- Validating findings against your group's morphological analyser output
- Co-writing the Methods + Discussion sections

Estimated commitment is ~30 hours over 4-6 months in 2029-2030.

**Practical next steps.** If either request is of interest, I would welcome a 30-minute video call at your convenience to discuss in more detail. If you would like to learn more first, the most accessible introduction to the project is the README at <https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/csl-observatory> and the design document at <https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/csl-observatory/blob/main/docs/OBSERVATORY_DESIGN.md>.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you.
